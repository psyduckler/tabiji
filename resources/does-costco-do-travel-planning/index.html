Does Costco Do Travel Planning? What Costco Travel Offers (and Where AI Planners Fill the Gaps)

Tabiji
February 13, 2026 · 10 min read

Does Costco Offer Travel Planning?

Yes, Costco offers travel planning through Costco Travel (costcotravel.com), a full-service online travel agency available exclusively to Costco members. Launched in 2000, Costco Travel lets members book vacation packages, cruises, hotels, rental cars, guided tours, and theme park tickets — often at below-market prices thanks to Costco's bulk purchasing power.

However, Costco Travel is a booking platform, not a personalized trip planner. It offers pre-built vacation packages and lets you bundle flights, hotels, and cars, but it doesn't create custom day-by-day itineraries, suggest activities based on your interests, or adapt plans in real time. For travelers who want a fully planned itinerary — not just booked hotels — AI-powered travel planners like tabiji.ai fill that gap.

What Costco Travel Actually Includes

Costco Travel provides six core services to members:

  • Vacation Packages — Bundled flights + hotels (+ optional car rentals) to popular destinations like Hawaii, Mexico, the Caribbean, and Europe. Many include Costco Shop Card bonuses worth $100–$500+.
  • Cruises — Partnerships with major cruise lines (Royal Caribbean, Norwegian, Celebrity) with member-exclusive pricing and onboard credits.
  • Hotels — Standalone hotel bookings at select partner properties, often with perks like complimentary breakfast or resort credits.
  • Rental Cars — Competitive rates through Avis, Budget, and Enterprise, with Executive Members getting additional discounts and free additional drivers.
  • Guided Vacations — Pre-planned group tours through partners like Trafalgar and Contiki, with expert guides, planned itineraries, transportation, and accommodations included.
  • Theme Park Packages — Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, and other parks bundled with hotels.

Membership Requirements and Costs

You must be a Costco member to use Costco Travel. There are two relevant tiers:

  • Gold Star ($65/year) — Access to all Costco Travel deals
  • Executive ($130/year) — All deals + 2% reward on travel purchases (up to $1,000/year back)

Executive Members booking a $5,000 vacation earn $100 back in rewards — effectively reducing their upgrade cost to $35.

Pros and Cons of Using Costco Travel

What Costco Travel Does Well

  • Competitive package pricing. Costco's bulk buying power often beats booking flights, hotels, and cars separately. A bundled Hawaii package can save $300–$800 compared to individual bookings on Expedia or direct hotel sites.
  • Valuable add-ons at no extra cost. Many packages include Costco Shop Cards ($100–$500+), complimentary breakfast, beverage credits, spa credits, or room upgrades.
  • Simplified booking. One transaction, one itinerary, one customer service number.
  • Trustworthy brand. Costco vets its travel partners the same way it curates warehouse inventory. Over 135 million members worldwide trust the brand.
  • 2% Executive rewards. Stacks with credit card rewards for double-dipping on points.

Where Costco Travel Falls Short

  • No standalone flights. You can only book airfare when bundled with a hotel or package.
  • Limited hotel and airline selection. Costco partners with a curated (small) set of properties and carriers.
  • No personalized itineraries. Costco Travel books your hotel and transport but doesn't plan what you'll actually do each day.
  • No real-time trip adjustments. If your plans change mid-trip, Costco Travel can't help you re-plan on the fly.
  • Mixed customer service reviews. Trustpilot reviews are polarized, with some travelers reporting difficulties with cancellations and changes.
  • Cancellation fees apply. Despite Costco's famously generous return policy on warehouse items, travel bookings may incur airline change fees and hotel cancellation penalties.

What Real Travelers Say About Costco Travel

Reddit discussions (r/Costco, r/travel, r/TravelHacks) reveal consistent themes:

  • Positive: Travelers consistently praise Costco Travel for Hawaii, Mexico, and Caribbean packages. Multiple users report saving "$1,000+" on Bora Bora, Maui, and Cancun trips. The included Costco Shop Cards are frequently cited as a deciding factor.
  • Mixed: Several Redditors note that Costco Travel isn't always cheaper — especially for Hawaii, where booking directly or through specialty agents can sometimes beat Costco's price. The advice: always price-compare.
  • Negative: Some travelers warn against Costco Travel for complex or multi-destination trips, recommending specialized travel planners instead. Customer service for changes and cancellations gets criticized.
  • Common gap cited: Even satisfied Costco Travel users note they still had to plan activities, dining, and day-to-day logistics separately.

Who Should Use Costco Travel?

Costco Travel is ideal if you:

  • Already have a Costco membership
  • Want an all-inclusive or resort-style vacation
  • Prefer one-click package booking over planning every detail
  • Are visiting popular destinations (Hawaii, Mexico, Caribbean, Las Vegas, Orlando)
  • Value added perks like Shop Cards and resort credits

Consider an AI travel planner instead (or in addition) if you:

  • Want a personalized day-by-day itinerary
  • Are visiting cities or regions where what you do matters as much as where you stay
  • Travel independently and want activity, dining, and logistics recommendations
  • Need flexibility to adjust plans during your trip
  • Are planning a multi-destination or complex route

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use Costco Travel without a membership?

No. Costco Travel is exclusively available to active Costco members (Gold Star, Executive, or Business). You'll need to enter your membership number to access pricing and book.

Does Costco Travel plan your itinerary?

Not in the traditional sense. Costco Travel books your flights, hotels, and transport, but it doesn't create a day-by-day activity plan. For guided vacations, the tour operator (e.g., Trafalgar) handles the itinerary. For independent trips, you'll need to plan activities yourself or use an AI travel planner.

Is Costco Travel cheaper than booking directly?

Often yes for bundled packages, especially to resort destinations. However, it's not universally cheaper. For standalone hotels or flights, direct booking or other OTAs sometimes offer better rates. Always compare prices before committing.

Does Costco Travel offer travel insurance?

Yes. Costco Travel partners with Zurich to offer optional travel insurance covering trip cancellation, medical emergencies, and baggage protection.

Can you earn airline miles through Costco Travel?

Yes. When flights are included in a Costco Travel package, you can still enter your frequent flyer number to earn miles on supported airlines.
