‚ö° Before You Go ‚Äî The Essentials

Getting There

Fly into Denver International (DEN) or Eagle County Regional Airport (EGE) ‚Äî EGE is 30 min from Vail and worth it if flights work out. From Denver, it's a scenic 2-hour drive west on I-70 through the Eisenhower Tunnel. Resort shuttles and private cars are also available from DEN.

Lift Tickets

Book lift tickets in advance online ‚Äî walk-up prices are brutal. An Epic Pass or Epic Day Pass gets you on the mountain; multi-day bundles are the best value. January is peak season so book tickets and lodging months ahead.

Where to Stay

Stay ski-in/ski-out in Vail Village or Lionshead for the full experience. The Sebastian Vail, Arrabelle at Vail Square, and Vail Mountain Lodge are standout options. Book early ‚Äî Vail in late January fills fast around MLK weekend (Jan 18) and the surrounding weeks.

January Conditions

January is Vail's best month. Average snowfall is 40+ inches for the season by this point, with fresh storms rolling through regularly. Highs range 25‚Äì35¬∞F (-4 to 2¬∞C). Powder days hit on average every 3‚Äì5 days. Layers are essential ‚Äî mornings are frigid, midday can feel warm in the sun.

Gear & Rentals

Rent from ski shops in Vail Village or Lionshead (Christy Sports, Vail Sports). If you're bringing your own gear, check ski bags into your hotel before hitting the village. Ski valets at most lodges will boot you up daily. Helmet: non-negotiable in January powder.

Mountain Layout

Vail has two base areas: Vail Village and Lionshead. The mountain spans from gentle front-side groomers all the way to the legendary Back Bowls (accessed via the back of the mountain). Blue Sky Basin is the farthest back ‚Äî the wildest, quietest terrain. Plan to explore all zones.

The Back Bowls ‚Äî China Bowl, Sun Down Bowl, Sun Up Bowl, and Tea Cup Bowl ‚Äî make up 2,700 acres of south-facing open terrain that is unlike anything else in North American skiing.
