üå§Ô∏è November in Ushuaia

Austral spring means long days (up to 17 hours of daylight), mild temperatures of 5‚Äì14¬∞C, and active wildlife. Penguins arrive at Martillo Island from late October. Weather is unpredictable ‚Äî pack waterproof layers, warm mid-layers, and sun protection.

üö¢ Getting There

Fly into Malvinas Argentinas International Airport (USH) from Buenos Aires (Aerolineas Argentinas, LATAM ‚Äî about 3.5 hours). Most travelers connect through Ministro Pistarini (EZE) or Jorge Newbery (AEP). Book flights early ‚Äî Ushuaia seats fill up in expedition season.

üß≠ Getting Around

Ushuaia city centre is walkable. Taxis and remises (private cars) are cheap and widely available. For Tierra del Fuego NP, either rent a car or take Bus Line 3 from downtown. Tour operators pick up from most hotels for excursions.

üêß Wildlife Notes

November is prime Magellanic penguin season at Martillo Island. Gentoo penguins are present year-round. Sea lions haul out at Isla de los Lobos. Bring binoculars and a telephoto lens if possible ‚Äî wildlife is abundant but approaches must respect distance rules.

The End of the Road ‚Äî Tierra del Fuego & Emerald Lake

Your final full day is the wildest: a morning hike to a hidden turquoise glacial lake hidden in lenga beech forest, then an afternoon in Tierra del Fuego National Park where you can stand at Lapataia Bay ‚Äî the official end of the Pan-American Highway, 17,848km from Prudhoe Bay, Alaska.
