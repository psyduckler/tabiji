‚ö° Before You Go ‚Äî Essentials

üöó Getting Around

A car is essential in Phoenix ‚Äî the metro area is massive and spread out. Rent one at Sky Harbor Airport (PHX) or use Uber/Lyft (rides are cheap, $10-20 between most spots). The Valley Metro light rail runs from the airport through downtown Phoenix to Tempe and Mesa, useful for Roosevelt Row and Mill Avenue but won't get you to trailheads.

ü•æ Hiking Tips

Start early ‚Äî trailheads fill up by 7-8am on weekends, and mid-day sun is intense even in February. Bring at least 1 liter of water per hour of hiking. Camelback's Echo Canyon trail is the most popular (and hardest) ‚Äî arrive before 6:30am for parking. Cholla Trail is slightly easier but still steep. South Mountain and Papago Park are more relaxed options.

‚òÄÔ∏è February Weather

Perfect conditions: 72-80¬∞F (22-27¬∞C) during the day, 48-55¬∞F (9-13¬∞C) at night. Rain is rare. The sun is intense at desert altitude ‚Äî sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses are non-negotiable even on 'cool' days. Mornings and evenings are ideal for outdoor activities. This is peak season, so popular spots get busy.

üíµ Budget Tips

Casual meals run $12-18, nice dinners $40-65pp. Happy hours are huge in Scottsdale ‚Äî many upscale restaurants offer half-price apps and drinks 3-6pm. Hiking is free (parking at Camelback is $5). Desert Botanical Garden is $25 but worth it, especially at golden hour. Spa day passes range from $50-150 depending on the resort.

üè® Where to Stay

Scottsdale Old Town for walkable dining, galleries, and nightlife. Downtown Phoenix (near Roosevelt Row) for arts and culture. Arcadia/Camelback corridor for proximity to hiking and a quieter residential vibe. If splurging, the resort corridor along Scottsdale Road has world-class spa properties with mountain views.

üì± Useful Apps

AllTrails (trail conditions and parking tips), Resy (restaurant reservations ‚Äî essential for popular Scottsdale spots), Uber/Lyft, Weather Underground (desert weather can shift fast), Google Maps offline (some trailhead areas have spotty signal).

‚úàÔ∏è Getting There

  • Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport (PHX) is centrally located, just 10 minutes from downtown. Direct flights from most major US cities.
  • Valley Metro light rail connects the airport to downtown ($2). Uber/Lyft to Scottsdale is about $15-20.

üöó Rental Car

  • Highly recommended ‚Äî the metro area spans 2,000+ square miles. Trailhead parking requires a car.
  • Rental cars at PHX are in a dedicated rental car center connected by shuttle. Book in advance during February peak season.

üåµ Desert Safety

  • Always carry water on hikes (1 liter per hour minimum). Wear sunscreen ‚Äî UV intensity is extreme.
  • Watch for rattlesnakes on trails (rare in February but possible). Stick to marked trails.
  • Desert plants like cholla cactus are painful on contact ‚Äî don't touch anything spiny.

üìÖ February Events

  • The Waste Management Phoenix Open (TPC Scottsdale) brings massive crowds ‚Äî book restaurants well in advance.
  • Spring training baseball starts late February at Cactus League stadiums across the Valley.
  • Check Scottsdale ArtWalk schedule (Thursdays 7-9pm).

üí° Pro Tips

  • Everything is farther apart than it looks on a map ‚Äî add 10-15 min buffer to drive times.
  • Many restaurants and bars have exceptional patios ‚Äî always request outdoor seating.
  • Arizona doesn't observe Daylight Saving Time (MST year-round).
  • Tap water is safe but tastes mineral-heavy ‚Äî bottled water is cheap everywhere.
