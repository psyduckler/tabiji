Getting There

Fly into Liberia (LIR) ‚Äî about 2.5 hours drive to Nosara. Or fly into San Jos√© (SJO) and take a domestic SANSA flight to Nosara's tiny airstrip (~35 min). Shuttle services from Liberia run $50‚Äì60/person. Rent a 4x4 if you want flexibility ‚Äî some roads are unpaved.

Many small restaurants and surf shops are cash-only. ATMs exist in Nosara but can run out. Withdraw colones at the airport. USD accepted at most tourist spots but you'll get better rates with colones. ‚Ç°1 USD ‚âà ‚Ç°510.

Late Feb Weather

Dry season perfection. Sunny, ~30¬∞C (86¬∞F), low humidity, almost zero rain. The best time to visit. Pack sunscreen (reef-safe!), a hat, light clothes, and a rashguard for surfing. Evenings are warm ‚Äî shorts and a tee are fine.

Getting Around

Nosara is spread out ‚Äî Playa Guiones, Playa Pelada, and the town center are all separate. A rental car or ATV is ideal. Otherwise, taxis and shared shuttles work. Many places are bikeable if you're up for dirt roads. Ask your hotel about bike rentals.

Surf Conditions

Late Feb is excellent for beginners and intermediates. Playa Guiones has consistent, forgiving beach breaks. Water is warm (~27¬∞C) ‚Äî no wetsuit needed, just boardshorts/bikini. Boards and lessons available everywhere along the beach.

Solo Travel Tips

Nosara is incredibly solo-friendly. Surf schools are social by nature ‚Äî you'll meet people immediately. Yoga studios and beach bars are the other hubs. Everyone here came to slow down, so conversations happen naturally. Don't overthink it.
